Tomato pizza ranges from authentic Italian styles, such as the cheese-less pizza marinara or pizza margherita using San Marzano tomatoes, to thick-crusted American tomato pies. Key features include high-quality, often uncooked or slightly cooked, tomato sauce, olive oil, and fresh herbs like basil. These pizzas are characterized by their simple, fresh, and often lower-calorie profile. Key Information & Characteristics Best Tomatoes: San Marzano plum tomatoes are considered ideal for their flavor, often hand-crushed or blended. Sauce Preparation: Many chefs prefer uncooked sauce for a fresher taste, or lightly simmering them with garlic, olive oil, and sometimes sugar to balance acidity. Crust: Options include thin, crispy, or thicker focaccia-style crusts, sometimes pre-baked or baked at very high temperatures (450°F+ / 230°C+). Toppings: While basic, popular additions include mozzarella (buffalo or fresh), fresh basil, garlic, oregano, and sometimes Parmesan or ricotta. Popular Varieties Pizza Marinara: A simple, vegan-friendly Italian pizza with tomatoes, garlic, herbs, and olive oil. Pizza Margherita: Features tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil. Tomato Pie (Philadelphia Style): Often served at room temperature with a thick, focaccia-like crust and a sweet, savory tomato sauce. Tips for Success Prevent Soggy Crust: Drain sliced tomatoes on a paper towel to remove excess moisture. Storage: The sauce can be made 4-5 days in advance and stored in the refrigerator. Cooking: If not using a pizza stone, baking at 200°C (390°F) for 6–8 minutes, or using a cast-iron pan on the stove, can produce a crisp result. A standard slice of tomato pizza is roughly 150-170 calories, making it a relatively light, traditional option.
